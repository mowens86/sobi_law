DUIs & Traffic Violations

If you or a loved one has been charged with a DUI or a DWI, immediate action should be taken. Once arrested you still have constitutional rights which are protected by the law.

You Only Have 10 Days From the Date of Your Arrest to Have Your DMV Hearing Requested, or Your Driver's License Will Be Suspended.

Your driver's license is an essential part of everyday life in California. Whether you're driving to and from work, need to go to the post office, or pick your kid's from school. You'll find that having your license revoked or suspended it'll make life much more inconvenient.

Once an arrest has been made for a DUI, the officer making the arrest can take away your license and give you a temporary 30-day license. After this expires your license will automtically expire. If convicted of a DUI in San Diego, you could not only lose your drivers license, but end up in jail, pay substantial fines, and deal with other court-ordered consequences.

Here's a few steps to follow if you've been arrested for a DUI:

1. Contact a DUI Lawyer or DUI Attorney

2. Contact the DMV ASAP to Start Protecting Your License

3. Make a Stay Request and a DMV Hearing

4. Obtain a Copy of the Police Report for Your Attorney to Review

5. Negotiate in Court to Resolve Your Case with the Best Options
